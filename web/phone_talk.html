Tech talk mirrors a big computer screen and covers both why and how.
  • Authentic, Us, Ours
    • Not accounts on services
    • Own our own keys
    • Bonus: no fake accounts, no bots
    • Facebook likes, Airbnb ratings, LinkedIn connections.. all of these are owned and live at those services and can only be trusted only when served by them.
    • Your keys, and not locked to any domain, not mine, not ONE-OF-US.NET's, not nerdster.org's...
      More on that in web examples and tech talk video.
Open
  • Let tech play nice togeter (ONE-OF-US.NET and the Nerdster are not connected)
  • Published statements are not trusted because of where they came from (what service served them)
    Their trusted because their signed by your own keys. Portable
  • We're using the ONE-OF-US.NET identity phone app but could use something else just as easily.
    • We're using Nerdster as a delegate service (could use 100s).
      We don't give delegate services our private identity key. We give them a crypto key pair and use our identity key to sign that it represents us.
  • Flash "Show Crypto" menu item, mentioned deferred to tech talk.
    • Crypto proof verfiable by anyone / any thing
      (Complete cryptographic signature chain to all of this data, all ours, no bots, all from your point of view.)
  • Follow / block for affinity contexts
    Identity layer establishes identity; affinity layer (any service) can leverage that how they like (evolution), eg. Trust a person for Airbnb rentals but block for NYT comments. We're complex people, but we are people.

Closing

A different paradigm (like the Internet was).

  • We build the network. We let them use it.
    Get us on the same page everywhere. Break the algorithm monopoly of our metadata silos.
  • Open = ours, everybody's
  • Decentralized = yours (No hierarchy, no power center.)
  • Litter the Internet with statements signed by our own keys grounded to our identities rather than pseudonymous and disconnected in siloed accounts.
