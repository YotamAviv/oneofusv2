1. What is the purpose of your app?

The Nerdster is a demo and reference implementation of the ONE-OF-US.NET paradigm (described in Q4).
It is a content rating and discovery app that leverages your decentralized identity network from
your own (or others') Point of View.

Available as a web app at https://nerdster.org. Docs and a demo video at https://one-of-us.net/.

2. What are the primary features of your app?

- Rate, submit, and comment on content (movies, articles, links, etc.).
- Follow people for different contexts (e.g. family, social) within your identity network.
- View content aggregated from your Point of View: only content your trusted network endorses surfaces to you.
- Sign in via the ONE-OF-US.NET identity app, which issues a service-specific delegate key to the
  Nerdster without ever sharing your identity key with it (see Q4).

Everything you do on the Nerdster publishes a statement cryptographically signed by your delegate
key, readable by any other service you sign in to with your public identity key.

Sign-in works in two ways:
- Different device (desktop/iPad + phone): Scan the Nerdster's QR code with the ONE-OF-US.NET app.
  The app creates and publishes a delegate key pair linked to your identity, then sends it to the Nerdster.
- Same device (iPhone): Tap a magic link on the sign-in screen, which opens the ONE-OF-US.NET app
  to complete the same flow and returns you to the Nerdster.

3. How does your app benefit the general public audience?

Your real-world trust network already exists. ONE-OF-US.NET writes it down cryptographically,
making it portable across services rather than locked in any silo. The Nerdster lets you leverage
that network: you see content endorsed by people you trust, not what an algorithm wants you to see.
No platform owns your identity or your ratings.

4. What is the ONE-OF-US.NET paradigm?

ONE-OF-US.NET is a decentralized identity paradigm. The phone app is your identity wallet: it
holds your key pair and lets you sign and publish statements — vouching for others ("This key
represents a person who is human, capable, and acting in good faith") or delegating to a service
("this delegate key represents me on nerdster.org"). Signed statements are published to the cloud
and verifiable by anyone. No central authority owns or controls them. The paradigm is
heterogeneous and non-proprietary: any service can participate.

content, follow people, etc. If removing bootstrap mode is acceptable, I will ship that version.
